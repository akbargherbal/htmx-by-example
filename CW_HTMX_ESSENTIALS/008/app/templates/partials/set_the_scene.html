Welcome to the University Registrar's Office!

In the world of web applications, the server acts much like a university's Registrar's Office. It's the central authority that manages requests, enforces rules, and maintains the official state of things.

When you try to register for a course, you're sending a request. If it's successful, the Registrar updates your schedule. If the course is full or you lack prerequisites, the Registrar sends back a specific reason for the denial. This is exactly how a server responds to your actions with different HTTP status codes (like 200 OK, 409 Conflict, or 403 Forbidden).

This lesson explores how HTMX elegantly handles these official "responses" from the server, whether it's a success, an error, or even a redirect to another "department" (like the bursar's office to pay tuition).
